I recently defended my doctoral thesis "Environmental policy for ecosystem services and biodiversity: Preferences for fish conservation and instruments for forest policy" at the Department of Forest Economics at the Swedish University of Agricultural Sciences (SLU). I am interested in how people try to make sense of the complexities and uncertainties of environmental science to arrive at relatively simple decisions about how to reduce their impact on the environment. My research generally addresses how to assist the public in converting their well-intentioned environmental ideas into effective behavior using public policy. I am currently teaching at SLU Umeå and working on projects with the Centre for Environmental and Resource Economics (CERE) at Umeå University.

Research interests: pro-environmental behavior, forest policy, discrete choice modeling, environmental economics, human dimensions of wildlife and natural resources
